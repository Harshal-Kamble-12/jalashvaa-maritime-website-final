# Maintenance Policy

The objective in maintaining a vessel is to protect the asset value and to operate the vessel without delays or stoppages at an economical cost level, without compromising the level of safety, and with due consideration for protection of the environment.

The Company’s policy is to:

- Maintain its vessels in full compliance of all SOLAS, IMO, Class, International and Flag State Regulations.
- Exercise due diligence in maintaining the vessels in a seaworthy and cargo worthy state at all times.

---

It is the Master and Chief Engineer’s responsibility on board the vessel to:

- Implement the Company’s policies and maintain the vessels in compliance with all regulations. It shall also be the Master’s responsibility to immediately report any defects affecting the safety of the vessel or its crew, to the office.
- Co-ordinate statutory surveys and inspections on board, once arranged with the relevant authority.
- Plan and requisition stores and spares necessary for maintenance. It is the Ship Manager’s responsibility to arrange for timely supply of materials required for maintenance.
- Identify and establish procedures for calibration of measuring and testing equipment, which is critical to the operation of the vessel.
- The Company will monitor the vessels’ condition through regular reports from the ship and by regular inspections by Shore based Managers.
