# Anti-Harassment and Bullying Policy

The Company is committed to:

- Protecting the dignity of all those who work within the Company.
- Ensuring that our vessels are free from any form of bullying or harassment and that our work environment is conducive to the employment of seafarers of all nationalities, culture, gender, religion, beliefs, and other diversities in an atmosphere of respect, safety and equality.

All employees have the right to be treated with dignity and respect. The Company does not discriminate against grounds of gender, sexual orientation or any other grounds prohibited by law.

No bullying or harassment within the Company, either on board or ashore will be tolerated. Complaints of bullying or harassment will be dealt with as described in our Crewing Manual.

Complaints by seafarers or other persons in the workplace of bullying or harassment at work will be treated with fairness, sensitivity, respect and (as far as possible) confidentiality for all parties concerned and referred to law enforcement authorities as applicable.

Both the Company’s management and its seafarers have responsibilities for creating and contributing to the maintenance of a work environment free from bullying and harassment.
Seafarers also have an obligation to cooperate with the investigation of complaints of bullying or harassment in the Company.

Managers, on board and ashore, have a particular responsibility to implement this policy and to make every effort to ensure that bullying and harassment does not occur, particularly in departments for which they are responsible.

Managers have an obligation to deal promptly and effectively with any incidents of bullying or harassment of which they are aware or ought to be aware.

The organization wants to ensure that the working environment supports an individual’s dignity and respect. Any act that is detrimental to an individual's dignity will be dealt with sternly. It will be the responsibility of the top management to ensure a safe working environment for employees.

---

All sexual harassment cases in India will be dealt with as per **The Vishakha Guidelines** promulgated by the Indian Supreme Court in 1997, superseded by the **Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013**. All records as per the Act are maintained with the Human Resource (HR) Manager in the Mumbai office.
