# Bunkering Policy

The Company recognizes the risk to the environment while bunkering and has put in place the following measures to eliminate this risk:

- No smoking or naked lights allowed in the vicinity of bunkering.
- Observance of local bylaws.
- No passengers / visitors allowed on board during bunkering.
- Only trained, qualified, and competent staff will oversee and conduct bunkering of the vessel.
- The Company nominates a dedicated employee (C/E) to supervise operations.
- Bunkering will only occur at suitable locations, during satisfactory weather conditions, and outside tidal windows.
- Vessels will be adequately moored and lines monitored by ship staff during bunkering.
- The Company bunkering checklist will be adhered to, signed, and records kept on file for three years.
- The Company has identified on-board details of relevant emergency services to contact in the event of a spillage.
- All relevant local and international rules and regulations will be complied with.

Detailed bunkering procedures to comply with this policy can be found in the Technical Operations Manual of the Company’s Safety Management System.
