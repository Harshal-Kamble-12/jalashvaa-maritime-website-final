

# Drug and Alcohol Policy

The Drug and Alcohol policy will apply to all personnel on board this Company’s fleet’s vessels. When ship is within port limits, all crew must strictly abide by the applicable local laws.

Company will cooperate fully with authorities in the prosecution of any crewmember possessing or under the influence of such substances. **Strict action including dismissal** will be taken against anyone violating, facilitating violations, or concealing violations.

Senior Management (**Master, C/E, C/O and 2/E**) is responsible for enforcing Company’s D & A policy on board.

All seafarers will be required to undergo Drug & Alcohol testing either prior to joining or as soon as possible thereafter. They may also be tested randomly during service on board by the Company or by the authorities.

---

## Following are strictly prohibited on board:

- Misuse of legitimate drugs
- Use, possession, distribution, sale or smuggling of illicit or non-prescription drugs or drug paraphernalia
- Bringing alcoholic beverage or drugs to the vessel either for consumption or taking home as a gift
- Procurement and sale of alcoholic beverages from any source including the ship’s bond locker
- Navigating a ship, assisting in navigation of a ship, operating its onboard equipment, carrying out repairs, keep engine room watch or carrying out any duty whilst impaired by drugs or alcohol
- Commencing scheduled duties with a blood alcohol concentration (**BAC**) more than **ZERO**

---

## Alcohol Consumption Restrictions

Seafarers in service (on board or on shore leave) shall not consume alcohol:

- At least **4 hours** prior to any scheduled watch keeping or work period
- More than **2.5 units of alcohol** as per the following table or have Blood Alcohol Concentration (**BAC**) of >0.025% (25 mg/100ml blood) at any time resulting in impairing their efficiency or judgment.

---

## Alcohol Unit Reference Table

| Beverage | Volume | Units |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Beer, cider, and lagers > 4.0%, < 6.0% Alc. by Vol. | 300ml | 2.5 |
| Beer, cider, and lagers > 1.0%, < 4.0% Alc. by Vol. | 300ml | 1.0 |
| Beer, cider, and lagers > 0.05%, < 1.0% Alc. by Vol. | 300ml | 0.5 |
| Wine > 6.0%, < 12.0% Alc. by Vol. | 100ml | 1.0 |
| Fortified wines > 12.0%, < 16.0% Alc. by Vol. | 60ml | 1.0 |
| Spirits and liqueurs > 16.0%, < 40.0% Alc. by Vol. | 30ml | 1.0 |
| Any other low alcohol beverage > 0.05%, < 1.0% Alc. by Vol. | 300ml | 0.5 |

---

Any Seafarer reporting for work or watch keeping appearing to be under influence of alcoholic beverages must undergo BAC test and shall not commence work or take over watch. **Master shall be informed at once.**

All Company officials, supernumeraries and visitors on board the ship are subject to the same policy restriction.

Drug trafficking and smuggling including selling bonded stores on board is a serious criminal offence and will be dealt with in accordance with the law and will result in **dismissal without notice**.

Seafarer(s) on prescribed drugs must declare this fact to the Master and the Company upon joining the ship, or upon prescription, if prescribed during the period of service onboard.

Senior Management onboard should report D & A violation by a phone call to the Company / Designated Person followed by an e-mail message.

All crew members are obliged and authorised to report any D & A violation by contacting the Designated Person / Company.
